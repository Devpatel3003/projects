SARDAR VALLABHBHAI PATEL

1875 - 1950

IRON MAN OF INDIA

Vallabhbhai Patel was born in Nadiad, Gujarat, on October 31, 1875 (His birth anniversary is now observed as National Unity Day or Rashtriya Ekta Diwas). He hailed from a farmer’s family. In his early years, Patel was considered by many as an unambitious man destined for a commonplace job. However, Patel proved them wrong. He passed law examination, often studying himself, with borrowed books. Patel practised law at Godhra, Borsad, and Anand in Gujarat, after passing the bar examination. He earned the reputation of being a fierce and skilled lawyer.Patel had a dream to study law in England. Using his hard-earned savings, he managed to get a pass and a ticket to go to England. However, the ticket was addressed to ‘V.J. Patel’. His elder brother Vithalbhai also had the same initials as Vallabhai. Sardar Patel came to know that his elder brother too cherished a dream to go to England for studies. In keeping with concerns for his family’s honour (disreputable for an older brother to follow his younger brother), Vallabhbhai Patel allowed Vithalbhai Patel to go, in his place.

biographies


  • Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel was born on 31st October 1875 in Nadiad city of Gujarat.

  • Sardar Patel earned his degree in law from England and returned India to practice in Gujarat.

  • He was against the British Government and so refused all the posts offered by them.

  • Patel married Jhaverben Patel in 1891 and they had two children.

  • Sardar Patel was deeply affected by Mahatma Gandhi and became a follower of Gandhian principles.

  • He led the ‘No Tax Campaign’ for farmers in 1918 which was a peaceful campaign.

  • He was also imprisoned in 1930 for participating in Salt Satyagrah.

  • In 1931, Sardar Patel was elected the president of the Indian National Congress.

  • Patel was the first Deputy Prime Minister of India and served from 15th August 1947 to 15th December 1950.

  • Sardar Patel left the world on 15th December 1950 due to a massive heart attack.
